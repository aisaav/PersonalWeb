Michigan Research and Discovery Scholars

Resident living community for undergraduates seeking research opportunities as a first-year student

Second-Year Peer Advisor

After my positive experience my freshman year i decided to become a Peer Advisor for the program and lead the Ethics group to expand not only my perspective but also of the students I would be advising.

Under this role I mentored a group of 10 students throughout the research process, notably to acquire undergraduate research contracts through Undergraduate Research Opportunity Program (UROP) and create a poster for the MRADS Symposium.

Introduced students to the the interdisciplinary nature of research by facilitating a series of seminars with alumni and professors; structuring a curriculum for teaching students about Ethics in Real World Contexts. My goal for my students was to educate them about how ethics is applicable to almost any field study.

I also provided students with individualized guidance and assistance throughout the school year ensuring they had a support system for academic and personal success.

First-Year Student Resident

My freshman year I was part of this resident affiliate program to acquire research opportunities as an undergraduate.

As a first year I would attend alumni and research seminars as a part of the technology group and learned about big data, reinforcement and deep learning.

Ultimately my positive experience in the program led me to want to become an advisor for first year students my sophomore year and lead the Ethics group.
